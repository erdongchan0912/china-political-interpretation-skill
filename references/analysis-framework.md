# China Political Analysis Framework

Use this checklist to avoid shallow commentary and keep interpretation evidence-led.

## 1. Fact Layer
- List only verifiable facts first: what document/event, which institution, what action, what date.
- Separate document type: law, regulation, notice, opinion, meeting readout, speech.
- Capture exact policy verbs: establish, rectify, strengthen, pilot, standardize, punish.

## 2. Intent Layer
- Explain explicit policy goals.
- Infer likely implicit political goals from timing, wording, and institutional placement.
- Compare with prior campaigns to detect continuity vs course correction.

## 3. Textual and Semantic Shift Layer
- Compare with at least one historical peer text of the same document type.
- Extract wording changes in three buckets:
  - Added phrases (new priorities or new governance instruments).
  - Removed phrases (de-prioritized narratives or toned-down constraints).
  - Modifier intensity shifts (for example from hard restriction to normalized regulation).
- Interpret what the wording shift implies for policy style, political risk tolerance, and enforcement tone.

## 4. Power and Organization Layer
- Map principal actors: Party organs, State Council system, ministries, provincial executors.
- Identify who leads coordination and who executes.
- Flag whether changes imply centralization, recentralization, or delegated experimentation.

## 5. Implementation Layer
- Define implementation chain: center -> province -> city/county -> frontline unit.
- Identify bottlenecks: funding, cadre incentives, data systems, legal authority.
- Note where policy may stay rhetorical instead of operational.

## 6. Distribution Layer
- Identify likely beneficiaries and burdened groups.
- Assess regional asymmetry: first-tier vs lower-tier, coastal vs inland, pilot vs non-pilot.
- Track sectoral effects and second-order consequences.

## 7. Narrative Competition Layer
- Compare framing across source tiers: official primary text, party-state media, market media, external media.
- Mark where narratives diverge and test which divergence is evidence-backed.

## 8. Scenario Layer (3-12 months)
- Build at least three scenarios: baseline, acceleration, friction/stall.
- Define trigger indicators for each scenario.
- Assign confidence by evidence quality, not by writing confidence.
- Add one mandatory extreme scenario with probability <10% but severe downside.

## 9. Business and Compliance Translation Layer
- Convert political signal into business impact by sector, ownership type, and value-chain role.
- Output operational stance for each key stakeholder:
  - brake: reduce exposure and freeze expansion.
  - watch: maintain operations with high-frequency monitoring.
  - capture: proactively align and seek policy dividend.
- Specify regulator engagement map: which authority to approach, what dossier to prepare, and what timeline to follow.

## Evidence Tagging Convention
- `F`: direct fact with source and date.
- `I`: inference from two or more facts.
- `S`: scenario judgment on future direction.

## Source Authority Tiers
- `T0`: core communiques and top-leadership authoritative speeches.
- `T1`: central normative documents and Xinhua authorized releases.
- `T2`: ministry regulations/circulars and central media commentary.
- `T3`: local implementation files and peripheral official interpretation.

## Minimum Delivery Standard
- Provide at least one vertical lineage chain with concrete time anchors.
- Provide at least one horizontal comparison table with at least three source types.
- Provide explicit uncertainty statements and verification plan.
- Provide one textual semantic diff table (added/removed/modifier shifts).
- Provide one business/compliance action section with brake/watch/capture recommendation.
